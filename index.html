      Harvard Medical School and Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH)

      Research Intern, January 2021 - Present

      I am a part-time research intern at Dr. Mohammad Jalali's Data and Systems Science in Public Health lab (MJ Lab). I help develop and utilize simulation models and data science methods to understand complex public health issues and to inform decision and policy making. I also help write research proposals for grants from the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), National Institutes of Health (NIH), and additional lab research funders.

      Most recently, I have been working on a project involving data mining illegal drug sales within the U.S.

      • MJ Lab

      MIT Sloan School of Management

      Undergraduate Research Assistant, June 2020 - Present

      Throughout my time at MIT Sloan, I have been a co-researcher on a two-year long survey analysis study led by Dr. David Keith and Dr. Joanna Moody on finding the option value of owning a car. As part of the research process, I built models using discrete choice modeling and regression and presented my findings to the MIT Energy Initiative Mobility Systems Center Research Team. Using our findings, I co-authored an economic research paper currently under final review for publication by Nature Sustainability. An article about my work published in MIT Sloan Press is linked below.

      Most recently, I have been analyzing survey data collected at the beginning of the coronavirus pandemic using various types of regression (poisson, binary, logistic, ordinal, etc.) and data analysis to find human behavioral trends.

      • MIT Sloan Press

      Tech Goes Home

      Business and Data Intern, Summer 2020 Internship

      For my summer 2020 internship, I worked at Tech Goes Home, a non-profit organization commited to digital equity around Boston, MA. As a Business and Data Intern, I conducted 20+ interviews, oversaw the work of 200+ volunteers, considered data collection/usage, and worked on improvements to the system as a whole. For my summer long internship project, I analyzed company trends in 23,000 participants primary languages against key variables of interest using Salesforce and R. At the end of my internship, I presented my findings and final project to the company CEO’s and entire TGH Team. I compiled my results for company board meetings and future use.

      • Tech Goes Home
